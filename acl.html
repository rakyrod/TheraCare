Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL)


The Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) is a crucial ligament in the knee joint that connects the femur (thigh bone) to the tibia (shin bone). It plays a key role in stabilizing the knee, preventing excessive forward movement of the tibia and controlling rotational forces. ACL injuries are common in sports that involve sudden stops, pivots, or jumps. A torn ACL can lead to pain, swelling, and knee instability, often requiring rehabilitation or surgical intervention for full recovery.
